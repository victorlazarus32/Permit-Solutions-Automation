What Is Included For Each Permit

Every one of these permits has to be worked individually. Many date back to the 1990s, and the county cannot pull them by expiration date, so each one is chased by its application reference. For each permit we:

  • Pull the full permit record and original application from Miami-Dade County archives. Older permits are on microfilm and have to be requested and retrieved by hand.
  • Review the file to pinpoint exactly what is open or missing, such as a final inspection that was never completed, an expired application, or a required revision.
  • Meet in person with the correct trade department for that permit (Building, Electrical, Mechanical, or Public Works). Each trade is a separate department and a separate desk, so each permit can require its own visit.
  • Confirm the exact close-out path with that department: renewal and inspection, a revision, a recertification, or close-out by affidavit.
  • Prepare and submit everything that department requires to reopen and process the permit.
  • Schedule and meet the inspector for any required inspection.
  • Track the permit through to a final, closed status and confirm it is cleared in the county system.
  • Provide you written confirmation once the permit is officially closed.
Government costs are separate. The fee above is for our professional service. County renewal and re-permit fees, re-inspection fees, and any engineering letters or affidavits the county requires to close a permit are billed separately at cost. We tell you each cost in advance before anything is paid.
